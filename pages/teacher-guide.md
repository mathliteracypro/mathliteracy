# 👩‍🏫 MathLiteracy Pro: Teacher & Educator Manual

Welcome, Educator! This manual is designed to guide you through managing your classes, tracking student enrollment metrics, publishing new curriculum topics, and reviewing student submissions.

---

## 🖥️ Teacher Dashboard & Classroom Insights

Educators on MathLiteracy Pro are equipped with a high-fidelity analytics dashboard that consolidates student metrics across various classes:

1. **Active Enrollment Counters**: Track how many students are actively working through specific CAPS topics (e.g., Financial Mathematics vs. Data Handling).
2. **Interactive Stats Audit Table**: Located on the **Sitemap Directory** or under the specialized audit page, this system compiles live statistics directly from your class databases. It aggregates:
   - Total subtopic video durations
   - Active subtopic lesson counts
   - Real-time student enrollments for each topic
3. **SBA Progress Indicators**: Monitor which students are staying on track with their School-Based Assessments (SBA).

## 📖 Managing Course Curriculum & Lessons

MathLiteracy Pro makes it incredibly simple to align your digital content with South African CAPS guidelines:

- **Create Topics**: Create overarching course categories mapped directly to Grade 10, 11, or 12 levels.
- **Set CAPS Weightings**: Assign official weightings to curriculum sections (e.g., Finance constitutes 35% of the annual paper weight) so students understand high-priority focal points.
- **Publish Subtopics & Video Content**: Attach YouTube URL links or custom MP4 assets, write rich-text markdown notes with embedded math symbols, and publish materials instantly.

## ✍️ Grading Homework & Providing Feedback

When students upload completed PDF spreadsheets or handwritten math sheets for their practice tasks, you will grade them from your teacher account:

### Grading Workflow
1. Navigate to the **Practice Tasks** board.
2. Select any task to view the list of submitted student responses.
3. Open student PDF or image attachments to review their formulas, calculations, and final answers.
4. Input their numeric score directly into the grade input block.
5. Write constructive, personalized feedback (e.g., *"Excellent work adjusting for interest rates, but double-check your compound interest exponent formula"*).
6. Click **"Save Grade"** to instantly push a system notification to the student's dashboard.

## 🎓 Verifying Course Certificates

Students can export official, PDF-ready **MathLiteracy Pro Completion Certificates** once they complete all modules in a syllabus topic. 

Each certificate is stamped with a unique, cryptographically secure validation hash. You can easily verify if a certificate is authentic by:
- Entering the hash into our public validator page: `https://mathlit.free.nf/verify/{hash}`
- Or clicking the **Verify Link** on their profile to view the live database validation record.
